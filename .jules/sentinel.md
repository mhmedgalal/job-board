## 2024-05-24 - Open Mail Relay in Contact Form
**Vulnerability:** Open Mail Relay / Email Spoofing (CWE-693 / CWE-77). The contact form in `contact/views.py` used the `email` field provided by the user as the `recipient_list` argument for `send_mail()`. This allowed attackers to send spam or malicious emails to arbitrary recipients via the application's email server.
**Learning:** Contact forms typically accept an email from the user to denote *who sent* the message. This email should be included in the email body or headers (like `Reply-To`), but it must not be used as the *destination* of the email. Destination emails for contact forms should be hardcoded to an administrator or support email.
**Prevention:** Always ensure that user-provided email addresses in contact forms are not used as the destination (`recipient_list`) for `send_mail`. Set the recipient to a known, configured internal address, such as `settings.DEFAULT_FROM_EMAIL`. Additionally, sanitize the `subject` to prevent header injection.
